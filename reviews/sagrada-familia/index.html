Sagrada Família: Our Review

By BeforeDepart Editorial Team · Updated June 2026
5
★★★★★
BeforeDepart Editorial rating · out of 5

Gaudí's unfinished masterpiece divides opinion from outside — but the interior left us speechless. Our review.

What it is

The Sagrada Família is Antoni Gaudí's extraordinary basilica, under construction since 1882 and still unfinished. Its towering, organic forms make it unlike any other church in the world.

The experience

The exterior is striking but busy; it's the interior that astonishes. Sunlight pours through stained glass that washes the soaring, tree-like columns in colour — it genuinely takes your breath away. It is, for us, one of the most beautiful interiors anywhere.

What we loved

  • The light-filled interior is simply stunning — unlike anything else.
  • Gaudí's nature-inspired design rewards slow, careful looking.
  • An audio guide adds genuine depth to the visit.

What to watch out for

  • It sells out — you must book a timed ticket ahead.
  • Ongoing construction means some areas and views are obscured.
  • Tower access costs extra and books up fast.

Is it worth it? Our verdict

Without hesitation — this is Barcelona's must-see, and the interior alone justifies the trip. Book a morning slot for the best light through the eastern windows. We give it a rare 5 out of 5.

Practical tips

  • Book a timed ticket online — it regularly sells out.
  • Morning light through the east windows is spectacular.
  • Add the audio guide; consider tower access if available.

Frequently asked questions

Is the Sagrada Família worth visiting inside?

Yes — the interior is the highlight and, for many visitors, one of the most beautiful spaces they've ever seen. Don't settle for viewing it only from outside.

Do you need to book Sagrada Família tickets?

Yes — timed tickets are essential and regularly sell out, so book online in advance.

What time of day is best to visit?

Morning, when sunlight streams through the eastern stained glass and floods the interior with colour.
