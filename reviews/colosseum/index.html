The Colosseum: Worth the Hype? Our Review

By BeforeDepart Editorial Team · Updated June 2026
4.5
★★★★½
BeforeDepart Editorial rating · out of 5

Two thousand years of history in one arena — we review whether Rome's Colosseum lives up to its legend.

What it is

The Colosseum is Rome's vast ancient amphitheatre, once host to gladiator games before tens of thousands of spectators. A ticket typically also covers the neighbouring Roman Forum and Palatine Hill.

The experience

Standing inside, the scale and engineering are genuinely moving — you feel the weight of history. Combined with the Forum next door, it's an unmissable half-day. The catch is crowds and queues; without a timed ticket or early slot, lines can be long and the interior packed.

What we loved

  • The sheer scale and the sense of standing inside living history.
  • One ticket also covers the Roman Forum and Palatine Hill.
  • Beautifully lit and photogenic in the early morning and at dusk.

What to watch out for

  • Long queues and crowds without a pre-booked slot.
  • Limited shade — brutal in summer heat.
  • Persistent ticket touts outside; buy official tickets only.

Is it worth it? Our verdict

Absolutely — it's one of the world's great monuments and a highlight of any Rome trip. Book a timed entry, go early, and pair it with the Forum. We give it 4.5 out of 5, docked only for crowds and summer heat.

Practical tips

  • Book a timed ticket online and arrive early.
  • Bring water, sun protection and comfortable shoes.
  • Allow half a day to include the Forum and Palatine Hill.

Frequently asked questions

Do I need to book Colosseum tickets in advance?

Yes — timed tickets are strongly recommended and often required, and they let you skip the worst of the queues.

Does the Colosseum ticket include the Roman Forum?

Usually yes — a standard ticket typically covers the Colosseum, Roman Forum and Palatine Hill. Check current ticket types when booking.

How long do you need at the Colosseum?

Around an hour inside the Colosseum, or half a day if you include the adjoining Forum and Palatine Hill.
