Is a Dubai Desert Safari Worth It? Our Review

By BeforeDepart Editorial Team · Updated June 2026
4.5
★★★★½
BeforeDepart Editorial rating · out of 5

Dune-bashing, camels and a desert sunset — we review whether the classic Dubai desert safari is worth booking.

What it is

A desert safari is a half-day trip into the dunes outside Dubai, typically mixing dune-bashing in a 4x4, sandboarding, camel rides and a Bedouin-style camp dinner with entertainment at sunset.

The experience

It's touristy but genuinely fun. The drive over the dunes is a thrill, the desert at sunset is beautiful, and the camp dinner is a relaxed, atmospheric end to the day. Quality varies a lot by operator, so the experience depends on choosing well.

What we loved

  • Exhilarating dune-bashing and gorgeous desert sunset light.
  • A genuine change of scene from the city's towers and malls.
  • The camp dinner and stargazing make a memorable evening.

What to watch out for

  • Very touristy, and quality varies between operators.
  • Dune-bashing can be too bumpy for some — not ideal if you get motion sick.
  • The cheapest tours can feel rushed and crowded.

Is it worth it? Our verdict

Yes — it's one of Dubai's signature experiences and great for families and first-timers. Pick a well-reviewed operator, go for the sunset slot, and it's a highlight. We give it 4.5 out of 5.

Practical tips

  • Choose a reputable, well-reviewed operator over the cheapest.
  • Book the late-afternoon/sunset slot.
  • Skip a big lunch beforehand if you're prone to motion sickness.

Frequently asked questions

Is a Dubai desert safari worth it?

Yes — especially the sunset trips with a reputable operator. It's a fun, scenic break from the city and great for families.

Is dune-bashing safe?

With a licensed, experienced operator it's safe, though bumpy. If you're prone to motion sickness, eat lightly beforehand and tell your driver.

What should I wear on a desert safari?

Light, comfortable clothing for the day and a layer for the evening, as the desert cools quickly after sunset.
