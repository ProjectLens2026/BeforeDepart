✈️ How to Survive a Long-Haul Flight

By BeforeDepart Editorial Team · Updated June 2026 · 6 min read

Beat jet lag and arrive fresh — simple, proven tips for flights over 6 hours.

Quick facts: Flying · 6 min read · Updated June 2026. Practical, original travel advice you can use on any trip.

Before you fly

A comfortable long-haul flight starts on the ground. Choose your seat in advance (a window for sleeping, an aisle for legroom and movement), wear loose layers, and start shifting your sleep schedule a day or two toward your destination's time zone.

Stay comfortable in the air

  • Drink plenty of water and go easy on alcohol and caffeine — cabin air is very dry.
  • Get up and walk or stretch every couple of hours to keep circulation moving.
  • Use a neck pillow, eye mask and earplugs or noise-cancelling headphones.
  • Set your watch to the destination time and sleep accordingly.

Beating jet lag

Get natural daylight at your destination as soon as you can — it is the fastest way to reset your body clock. Try to stay awake until a normal local bedtime on arrival day, and keep the first day's plans light.

Handy extras

  • Compression socks for very long flights.
  • Lip balm and moisturizer for dry cabin air.
  • Your own snacks, and a downloaded playlist or shows.

Do these and you will step off the plane far closer to ready than wrecked.
