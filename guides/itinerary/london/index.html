🗓️ 3 Days in London: The Perfect Itinerary

By BeforeDepart Editorial Team · Updated June 2026

A practical, easy-to-follow 3-day plan for London that covers the highlights without burning you out — adjust it to your own pace.

📘 Full London guide →📍 All things to do →

Day 1: Landmarks & first impressions

  • The British Museum and the National Gallery (both free).
  • Markets: Borough for food, Camden for character.

Pace yourself, leave room to wander, and don’t over-schedule.

Day 2: Culture, neighbourhoods & food

  • A walk from Westminster along the South Bank to the Tower of London.
  • A green afternoon in Hyde Park or Regent's Park.

Pace yourself, leave room to wander, and don’t over-schedule. End the day with dinner at a local spot — London's food is gloriously global — curry houses, dim sum, Middle Eastern grills, and modern British cooking.

Day 3: A relaxed finish or a day trip

  • A ride on the London Eye for the view.

Pace yourself, leave room to wander, and don’t over-schedule.

Where to stay in London

Westminster and South Bank put you near the big sights. Kensington is elegant and near the free museums. Shoreditch and Camden are trendier and more affordable. Anywhere near a Tube station works well.

Getting around

The Underground ("the Tube") plus buses cover everything — just tap a contactless card or phone; fares are capped daily. Walking between central sights is often quicker than you think. Avoid driving: congestion charges and parking make it pointless.

Frequently asked questions

Is 3 days enough in London?

Three days is enough to enjoy the main highlights of London at a comfortable pace. Add a day or two if you want day trips or a slower rhythm.

What should I do on a first trip to London?

Focus on the signature sights first: The British Museum and the National Gallery (both free); A walk from Westminster along the South Bank to the Tower of London; A ride on the London Eye for the view.
