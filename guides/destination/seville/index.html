🇪🇸 Seville: Top Things to Do

By BeforeDepart Editorial Team · Updated June 2026 · 6 min read

Moorish palaces, orange-tree plazas and flamenco — sunny, romantic and wonderfully walkable.

The soul of Andalusia

Seville is warm, atmospheric and steeped in Moorish and Spanish history, with grand monuments and lively tapas streets. The center is compact and made for slow, sunny wandering.

Best time to visit

March–May and September–October are ideal. Summer is extremely hot (often 40°C+), so plan around the heat. Spring brings beautiful weather and famous festivals.

Getting around

The historic center is very walkable; a tram and buses help, and bikes are popular. No car needed in the center.

Where to stay

Santa Cruz, the old Jewish quarter, is central and charming. El Arenal is near the river and bullring. Alameda is trendier and more local.

What to eat

Tapas are a way of life here — graze across several bars. Try local specialties and chilled gazpacho in summer. Vegetarian options are increasingly common; BeforeDepart's finders also map Halal and kosher spots.

Top things to do

  • The Royal Alcázar palace and gardens.
  • Seville Cathedral and the Giralda tower.
  • The stunning Plaza de España.
  • Wander the lanes of Santa Cruz.
  • An evening flamenco performance.

Safety & money

Seville is safe; use normal caution against pickpockets in busy areas. The currency is the euro; cards are widely accepted. Emergency number is 112.
