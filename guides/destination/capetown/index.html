🇿🇦 Cape Town: Top Things to Do

By BeforeDepart Editorial Team · Updated June 2026 · 6 min read

Table Mountain, dramatic coastlines and winelands — one of the world's most beautiful cities.

Quick facts: South Africa · 6 min read · Updated June 2026. This guide covers when to go, getting around, where to stay, what to eat, the top things to do, and safety & money for Cape Town.

A city framed by nature

Cape Town sits between a flat-topped mountain and two oceans, with beaches, vineyards and dramatic drives all close by. Build your days around the weather, since the mountain and coast are best on clear, calm days.

Best time to visit

Remember seasons are flipped: summer (November–March) is warm, dry and best for beaches and hiking. Winter (June–August) is cooler and wetter but green and quiet. Spring brings wildflowers.

Getting around

A rental car or ride-hailing apps are the practical ways to get around, as public transport is limited for visitors. Many sights are spread out along the coast and into the winelands.

Where to stay

The V&A Waterfront and City Bowl are central and convenient. Camps Bay and Sea Point are scenic and beachy. Choose based on whether you want city access or coastal views.

What to eat

Enjoy fresh seafood, South African braai (barbecue), and Cape Malay curries. The winelands are world-class. Vegetarian and Halal options are easy to find; BeforeDepart's finders map them.

Top things to do

  • Table Mountain by cable car or hike (weather permitting).
  • The Cape Peninsula drive to Cape Point.
  • Boulders Beach to see the penguins.
  • A day in the Stellenbosch or Franschhoek winelands.
  • The V&A Waterfront and a trip to Robben Island.

Safety & money

Cape Town is stunning but take safety seriously: avoid displaying valuables, use ride-hailing apps at night, don't walk alone after dark, and check local advice on which areas to avoid. The currency is the rand; cards are widely accepted. Emergency number is 10111 (police) / 10177 (ambulance).
