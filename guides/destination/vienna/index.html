🇦🇹 Vienna: Top Things to Do

By BeforeDepart Editorial Team · Updated June 2026 · 6 min read

Imperial palaces, coffee houses and classical music — elegant, walkable and spotlessly easy.

Quick facts: Austria · 6 min read · Updated June 2026. Covers when to go, getting around, where to stay, what to eat, the top things to do, and safety & money for Vienna.
📍 Things to do in Vienna →🗓️ 3 days in Vienna →

Old-world elegance

Vienna is grand, orderly and steeped in imperial history and music, yet compact and easy to explore. Pair palace visits with a slow afternoon in a traditional coffee house — a local ritual.

Best time to visit

April–May and September–October are ideal. Summer is warm and lively; December is magical with Christmas markets, though cold. Winters are chilly but festive.

Getting around

Public transport (U-Bahn, trams and buses) is excellent and punctual; the historic center is very walkable. A car is unnecessary.

Where to stay

The 1st district (Innere Stadt) puts you among the sights but costs more. The 7th (Neubau) is trendy and central. Anywhere near a U-Bahn line is convenient.

What to eat

Try Wiener schnitzel, sachertorte and apple strudel, and linger over coffee and cake. Vegetarian options are easy; BeforeDepart's finders also map Halal and kosher spots.

Top things to do

  • Schönbrunn Palace and its gardens.
  • The historic center, St. Stephen's Cathedral and the Hofburg.
  • A classical concert or opera evening.
  • The art at the Belvedere or Kunsthistorisches Museum.
  • A relaxed afternoon in a Viennese coffee house.

Safety & money

Vienna is one of the safest cities in the world; normal city sense is plenty. The currency is the euro; cards are accepted widely, though some cafes prefer cash. Emergency number is 112.
