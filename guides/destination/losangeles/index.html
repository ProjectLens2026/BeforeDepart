🇺🇸 Los Angeles: Top Things to Do

By BeforeDepart Editorial Team · Updated June 2026 · 6 min read

Beaches, Hollywood, and sunny sprawl — how to enjoy LA without spending all day in the car.

Quick facts: United States · 6 min read · Updated June 2026. This guide covers when to go, getting around, where to stay, what to eat, the top things to do, and safety & money for Los Angeles.

Making sense of the sprawl

Los Angeles is huge and spread out, so the secret is to cluster your days by area — the coast one day, Hollywood and the hills another — instead of crisscrossing the city. Accept that you will spend some time driving, and plan around traffic.

Best time to visit

March–May and September–November bring warm, pleasant weather and fewer crowds. Summer is hot inland but lovely at the coast. Winters are mild. Mornings can be cloudy near the beach ("June gloom").

Getting around

A car is the practical way to get around LA, though the Metro covers some routes and ride-hailing is easy. Build extra time for traffic, especially at rush hour. Within neighborhoods like Santa Monica or Hollywood, walking works.

Where to stay

Santa Monica or Venice for the beach. Hollywood or West Hollywood for nightlife-free dining and sights. Downtown for museums and walkability. Pick based on what you most want to do, to limit driving.

What to eat

LA is a food paradise — world-class tacos, Korean BBQ, sushi and fresh, health-focused fare. Vegetarian, vegan and Halal options are abundant across the city; BeforeDepart's finders map them by neighborhood.

Top things to do

  • The Griffith Observatory for views and the Hollywood Sign.
  • Santa Monica Pier and a beach-path bike ride to Venice.
  • The Getty Center's art and gardens.
  • A studio tour or a theme park (Universal, or Disneyland nearby).
  • A walk along Hollywood Boulevard.

Safety & money

LA is fine with normal big-city awareness; areas vary block by block, so check before wandering at night, and don't leave valuables in parked cars. Tipping is expected. Cards are accepted everywhere. Emergency number is 911.
