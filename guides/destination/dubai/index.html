🇦🇪 Dubai: A First-Timer's Guide

By BeforeDepart Editorial Team · Updated June 2026 · 6 min read

The futuristic and the traditional, when to go to dodge the heat, getting around, etiquette, and the food worth seeking out.

Quick facts: United Arab Emirates · 6 min read · Updated June 2026. This guide covers when to go, getting around, where to stay, what to eat, the top things to do, and safety & money for Dubai.

What Dubai actually is

Dubai is a city of superlatives — the tallest tower, vast malls, man-made islands — but the trip people remember mixes the futuristic with the traditional: a morning in the creek-side souks and an evening watching the fountains under the Burj Khalifa. Plan for both sides and the city makes a lot more sense.

Best time to visit

November to March is ideal: warm, sunny days perfect for the beach and outdoor dining. April and October are hot but manageable. From June to September it is extremely hot (often 40°C+), so summer trips become an indoor, early-morning and after-dark affair — though hotel prices drop sharply.

Getting around

The Metro is clean, cheap and driverless, linking the airport, Downtown and Marina; buy a rechargeable Nol card. Taxis and ride-hailing apps are plentiful and affordable. Distances are big and it is too hot to walk far for much of the year, so build transport into every plan. A car is handy for day trips (the desert, or Abu Dhabi) but unnecessary in the city.

Where to base yourself

Downtown puts you by the Burj Khalifa, Dubai Mall and the fountains. Dubai Marina and JBR are best for beach, waterfront dining and a buzzy atmosphere. Deira and Bur Dubai — the older districts around the Creek — are cheaper, more traditional, and great for souks and first-hand culture.

What to eat

Dubai's food scene is global — Emirati, Lebanese, Indian, Persian and more. Have a shawarma and fresh juice from a local spot, try a traditional Emirati meal at least once, and don't overlook the excellent South Asian restaurants in the older neighborhoods. Almost everything is halal; vegetarian and vegan choices are easy thanks to the strong Indian influence. BeforeDepart's finders pinpoint them for you.

Don't-miss experiences

  • Sunset from the top of the Burj Khalifa, then the fountain show below.
  • A desert safari with dune driving and a Bedouin-style dinner.
  • An abra (small wooden boat) across Dubai Creek to the gold and spice souks.
  • A slow afternoon on the beach at JBR or Kite Beach.

Etiquette, safety & money

Dubai is one of the safest big cities in the world, with very low street crime. Dress is relaxed at beaches and hotels, but cover shoulders and knees in malls, souks and public areas, and be discreet with public affection. Alcohol is served in licensed venues such as hotels. The currency is the UAE dirham (AED) and cards are widely accepted. Emergency numbers are 999 (police) and 998 (ambulance).
