🇺🇸 Orlando: Family Travel Guide

By BeforeDepart Editorial Team · Updated June 2026 · 6 min read

The theme-park capital — how to plan the parks, save money, and keep the whole family happy.

Quick facts: United States · 6 min read · Updated June 2026. This guide covers when to go, getting around, where to stay, what to eat, the top things to do, and safety & money for Orlando.

Planning the parks

Orlando is built for families, with Walt Disney World, Universal and more. The biggest mistake is winging it — the parks reward planning. Decide which parks matter most, book key tickets and dining in advance, and build in rest so nobody melts down by day three.

Best time to visit

Late January to early March and September to early November bring smaller crowds and milder weather. Summers are hot, humid and busy, with afternoon thunderstorms. Avoid major US holidays for the longest lines.

Getting around

A rental car is the most flexible option for getting between resorts and parks; many hotels also offer shuttles. Ride-hailing is easy. Distances are large and walkable areas are limited to inside the parks and resorts.

Where to stay

On-property Disney or Universal hotels add convenience and perks. Areas like Lake Buena Vista and International Drive offer lots of family hotels with pools, often at better prices and near both park clusters.

What to eat

Beyond theme-park food, Orlando has huge variety, from casual chains to global cuisine along International Drive. Book popular park restaurants ahead. Vegetarian, vegan and Halal options exist across the parks and city — BeforeDepart's finders help you locate them.

Top things to do

  • Magic Kingdom and EPCOT at Walt Disney World.
  • Universal Studios and Islands of Adventure (great for older kids).
  • A water park day to cool off.
  • Kennedy Space Center, about an hour east.
  • Mini-golf and dining at Disney Springs or CityWalk.

Safety & money

Orlando is a safe, tourist-focused city; the main risks are heat and exhaustion — hydrate and pace the kids. Tickets and food add up, so set a budget. Cards are accepted everywhere. Emergency number is 911.
