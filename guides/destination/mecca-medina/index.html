🇸🇦 Mecca & Medina: An Umrah Travel Guide

By BeforeDepart Editorial Team · Updated June 2026 · 7 min read

A practical guide for Umrah travelers — when to go, where to stay near the Haram, getting around, and what to know.

Quick facts: Saudi Arabia · 7 min read · Updated June 2026. This guide covers when to go, getting around, where to stay, what to eat, the top things to do, and safety & money for Mecca.

Before you plan

For Muslim travelers, a journey to the holy cities of Mecca and Medina is deeply meaningful. This is a practical travel guide to help with the logistics; always follow the religious requirements of Umrah and the latest official guidance from Saudi authorities, as rules and permits can change.

Best time to go

Umrah can be performed year-round. The cooler months (roughly November–February) are far more comfortable than the intense summer heat. Avoid the Hajj period for Umrah, and expect larger crowds during Ramadan, which many consider especially rewarding but very busy.

Entry & permits

Most travelers enter on an Umrah or tourist visa; requirements, health rules and any required permits change, so confirm the current process through official Saudi channels or an approved agent before booking.

Where to stay

In Mecca, staying as close as possible to the Masjid al-Haram makes repeated visits far easier, especially for elderly travelers. In Medina, choose a hotel near the Prophet's Mosque (Al-Masjid an-Nabawi). Central hotels cost more but save long walks in the heat.

Getting around

Both holy mosques are walkable from nearby hotels. Between the cities, the high-speed Haramain railway connects Mecca, Jeddah and Medina comfortably; buses and taxis are also available. Ride-hailing apps work in the cities.

Practical tips

  • Pack light, modest clothing and comfortable footwear; bring your ihram garments.
  • Carry a refillable water bottle and stay hydrated in the heat.
  • Keep medication, documents and some local cash in a small bag.
  • An eSIM keeps you connected for maps and contacting your group.
  • Travel insurance with medical cover is strongly recommended.

Money & essentials

The currency is the Saudi riyal; cards are widely accepted, with cash useful for small purchases. Respect local customs and dress codes throughout. Emergency number is 911.
