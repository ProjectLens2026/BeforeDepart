🇦🇪 Abu Dhabi: Top Things to Do

By BeforeDepart Editorial Team · Updated June 2026 · 6 min read

A calmer Gulf capital of grand mosques, museums and family attractions — safe and Halal-friendly.

Quick facts: United Arab Emirates · 6 min read · Updated June 2026. Covers when to go, getting around, where to stay, what to eat, the top things to do, and safety & money for Abu Dhabi.

The UAE's relaxed capital

Abu Dhabi is greener, calmer and more spacious than nearby Dubai, with world-class culture and family attractions. It is very safe and Halal-friendly, ideal for families.

Best time to visit

November to March is ideal — warm and comfortable. Summer (June–September) is extremely hot, so plan indoor attractions and after-dark outings. Spring and autumn evenings are pleasant.

Getting around

Taxis and ride-hailing apps are affordable and the easiest way around; distances are big and walking far is uncomfortable in the heat. A car helps for attractions spread across the city and islands.

Where to stay

The Corniche and city center for the waterfront and downtown. Yas Island for theme parks and family fun. Saadiyat Island for beaches and museums.

What to eat

Enjoy Emirati and wider Middle Eastern cuisine plus global options. Nearly everything is Halal, and vegetarian choices are common. BeforeDepart's finders pinpoint them.

Top things to do

  • The Sheikh Zayed Grand Mosque (dress modestly; it's free to visit).
  • Louvre Abu Dhabi on Saadiyat Island.
  • Yas Island theme parks (Ferrari World, Warner Bros.).
  • A walk or bike along the Corniche.
  • A desert safari among the dunes.

Safety & money

Abu Dhabi is extremely safe with very low crime. Dress modestly in public and at the mosque (covering shoulders and knees; abayas provided at the mosque for women). The currency is the dirham (AED); cards are widely accepted. Emergency number is 999.
