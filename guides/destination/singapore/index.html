🇸🇬 Singapore: Top Things to Do

By BeforeDepart Editorial Team · Updated June 2026 · 6 min read

Spotless, safe and family-friendly — futuristic gardens, hawker food, and a city that just works.

Quick facts: Singapore · 6 min read · Updated June 2026. This guide covers when to go, getting around, where to stay, what to eat, the top things to do, and safety & money for Singapore.

Why Singapore is so easy

Singapore is clean, safe, efficient and compact — one of the easiest big cities to visit, and especially great for families and first-time travelers to Asia. English is widely spoken and everything runs on time.

Best time to visit

It is hot and humid year-round near the equator, with brief daily showers possible anytime. February to April is slightly drier. There is no bad season; just plan indoor breaks during midday heat.

Getting around

The MRT metro is clean, cheap and reaches almost everywhere — tap a contactless card. The city is walkable in stretches, with lots of sheltered, air-conditioned walkways. Taxis and ride-hailing are reliable and reasonable.

Where to stay

Marina Bay for the iconic skyline and attractions. Chinatown and Bugis for character and value. Orchard Road for shopping. Everywhere is well-connected by MRT.

What to eat

Hawker centers are the heart of the food scene — cheap, delicious and safe. Try Hainanese chicken rice, laksa, satay and chili crab. Halal, vegetarian and vegan stalls are common; BeforeDepart's finders map them across the island.

Top things to do

  • Gardens by the Bay and the Supertree light show.
  • The Marina Bay waterfront at night.
  • Sentosa island for beaches and family attractions.
  • The Singapore Zoo or Night Safari.
  • A hawker-center food crawl.

Safety & money

Singapore is one of the safest cities in the world. Laws are strict (littering, jaywalking and chewing-gum rules are real), so follow the signs. The currency is the Singapore dollar; cards are accepted nearly everywhere. Emergency number is 999 (police) / 995 (ambulance).
