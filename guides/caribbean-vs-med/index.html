🌊 Caribbean vs Mediterranean Cruise: Which Is Right for You?

By BeforeDepart Editorial Team · Updated June 2026 · 6 min read

Beaches or history? We compare the two most popular cruise regions to help you pick the right one.

Quick facts: Cruises · 6 min read · Updated June 2026. Practical, original travel advice for any trip.

Two very different trips

The Caribbean and the Mediterranean are the world's most popular cruise regions — but they offer almost opposite experiences. One is about beaches and relaxation; the other is about history and culture. Here's how to choose.

The vibe

A Caribbean cruise is sun, sea and sand: turquoise water, beach days, and a relaxed pace. A Mediterranean cruise is port-intensive and cultural, packing in historic cities, ruins and art, with less beach time and busier days ashore.

Best time to go

The Caribbean is best in winter and spring (roughly December to April), escaping cold northern weather, with hurricane season later in the year. The Mediterranean shines from late spring to early autumn (May to September), though midsummer is hot and crowded.

What you'll see

  • Caribbean: islands like the Bahamas, St. Maarten and Cozumel — beaches, snorkeling and watersports.
  • Mediterranean: cities like Rome, Barcelona, Athens and Santorini — landmarks, museums and old towns.

Who each suits

Choose the Caribbean if you want to switch off, lie on a beach, and keep things easy — it's also very family-friendly. Choose the Mediterranean if you love history and sightseeing and don't mind full, active days in port.

Cost

Caribbean cruises are often cheaper overall, with shorter flights for North American travelers and lower-cost excursions. Mediterranean cruises can cost more once flights and bigger-city excursions are factored in, but pack in far more sightseeing.

The verdict

For relaxation and beaches, sail the Caribbean. For history and culture, choose the Mediterranean. Either way, BeforeDepart's Cruises tab compares regions, lengths, costs and what's included so you can book with confidence.
